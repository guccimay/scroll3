About Scroll 3: On Site


Scroll 3: On Site offers a thorough excavation of a small
portion of Baltimore’s most influential yet uncelebrated
architecture; all of the spaces investigated still stand
today, though not all are active. Some have been transformed
into chain restaurants or corporate businesses, leaving
no trace of the unique projects they once housed.

Realizing that many arts conversations in Baltimore are
dominated by a single generation of voices, we set out
to interview cultural contributors from a variety of
generations. Our research spanned projects that have been
defunct for decades to projects inaugurated in the past year.
On Site utilizes eight case studies of properties that have
changed hands multiple times throughout their history to speak
to different aspects of Baltimore’s changing artistic,
financial,and cultural ecosystem. While we understand that
no single space can provide a comprehensive history of
Baltimore,our hope is that the projects explored begin to
provide some insight into the incredible projects that were
happening within Baltimore’s neighborhood in the 70s, 80s,
and 90s, as a way of contextualizing what is happening in
the present.

We have observed a peripheral blindness that occurs when
one’s understanding of a city is informed only by the brief
duration spent living there for school or work, and our hope
is that this issue of On Site acts as a reminder that even
the most innovatives ideas are built upon past experiments
and experiences.

Thank you to everyone who provided words, artifacts, photographs,
and memories of the various projects included in this issue of
Scroll. We hope that this publication functions as a catalyst to
further explore Baltimore’s extremely rich cultural landscape.
